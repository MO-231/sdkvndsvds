About Cookies!

Cookies were invented in the 1930s by a chef named Ruth Graves Wakefield at the Toll House Inn in Whitman, Massachusetts. She created the “Toll House Chocolate Crunch Cookie” to serve her guests.

Fun Fact: The word cookie comes from the Dutch word “koekje,” which means “little cake.”

Cookies are one of the most iconic treats in the world. They are small, sweet desserts made from flour, eggs, butter, and sugar. Cookies are popular because they are easy to make and customizable.

Our group worked hard to make cookies that are appealing in both taste and appearance. This website guides you through the ingredients and step-by-step procedure.

Ingredients

  1. All-purpose flour – used for baking cookies, cakes, and bread.
  2. Baking soda
  3. Unsalted butter (softened)
  4. White sugar
  5. Brown sugar
  6. 1 large egg
  7. Vanilla extract
  8. Chocolate chips or chunks

Procedure

Step 1. Mix ½ cup softened butter, ⅓ cup white sugar, and ½ cup brown sugar until smooth.

Step 2. Add 1 egg and 1½ teaspoons vanilla extract. Mix until fluffy.

Step 3. Add 1½ cups flour, 1½ teaspoons baking soda, and 1 teaspoon salt. Stir well.

Step 4. Add 1½ cups chocolate chips or chunks and mix.

Step 5. Scoop 12–13 balls of cookie dough.

Step 6. Place on a lined baking sheet.

Step 7. Bake at 350°F for 12–14 minutes.
